Bonestructure Answered:

I'm better off now than I was 4 years ago, but that's because I finally got my disability SSI. If not for that, and I count my blessings daily, I doubt I would be better off. Actually, I'd be dead, as without the medicaid that came with the SSI, I'd have died a couple of times already. And I did die once last January, but I was in the ER at the time. So I count my blessings as I say. But no, I don't think the poor are better off now. The big problem, which I do NOT blame of either Bush or Obama, is that jobs and opportunities have dwindled. If the best job you can get is flipping burgers at MacDonald's, Selling drugs or robbing people starts to look pretty good.

Bonestructure Answered:

Here's the problem with the whole welfare system. It's set up in such a way that they just never allow you to get ahead. Make a little money, they take it off your food or housing or whatever. So the harder you try, the less you can pull yourself out of poverty. With SSI, at least I'm able to make a little, as I'm able, and live a little bit better life. Of course, I live a relatively simple life, so I don't take much money. But it is nice when I sell art or a short story and get a few extra bucks here and there. Which usually goes to food or necessities or clothes or useful stuff. My only frivolities are movies and books, but I consider those necessities to keep my sanity intact. I don't get out much. But I really do think that the welfare system, which I consider very important, needs to be remade so that you can A. kick the asses of the people who are cheating the system, and B. have some kind of realistic options to allow people to pull themselves out of poverty. Because, maybe I'm naive, but I find it hard to believe any human being could be content or satisfied living in poverty.
